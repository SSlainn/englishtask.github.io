Intergalactic Kichta Ecology Agrifood

Welcome to the IKEA program page.

[I.K.E.A]

IKEA, a subsidiary of KSP, has been responsible for the logistics and management of the company's food needs for over 50 years. Its CEO, Nguyen Alexandre, has been at the helm of the company for over 15 years. He was appointed to head the company because of his experience in the field of logistics and food processing. This subsidiary, a key element, is essential to ensure good coordination and to guarantee the viability of the [K.S.P] project.

The researchers assigned to this subsidiary are all graduates of schools specialising in logistics and agronomy. Innovative solutions are therefore proposed in order to guarantee the viability of the project as well as its food and material self-sufficiency. On the food side, revolutionary devices developed in the IKEA laboratories guarantee the production of healthy and non-perishable foodstuffs. Indeed, these devices go beyond the concept of bland and depressing freeze-dried food, they are innovative, appetizing, and above all nutritious. The plants are produced in greenhouses equipped with lights that recreate the climatic conditions of the earth. As for the meat, it is provided by muscle cell cultures, thus making it possible to obtain healthy, nutritious meat that does not require animal husbandry. In order to be part of a recycling and waste revalorisation process, the waste from the production of these products, being organic, is either composted or simply incinerated in order to produce chemical fertilisers.

As for the material, the raw materials are loaded into pressurised containers allowing them to be stored properly. The vast majority of the furniture and tools used are made up mainly of metals, which guarantee their durability, unlike plastics, which wear out very quickly. The furniture and infrastructure are modular and each part can be manufactured within the space station using automatic machines, which means that defective parts can be replaced quickly. The computerisation of production catalogues and assembly plans ensures that products can be produced quickly and efficiently to meet the station's needs.

Our vocation is to go further and further, with you, towards the future and beyond.

Please believe in my unwavering determination to meet the challenge that humanity will have to face.
